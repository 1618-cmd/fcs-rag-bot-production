Product UpdatesVena Copilot’s native integration with Microsoft Teams brings powerful AI capabilities right into
your daily workflow. Whether you’re in finance, planning or a business unit, you can interact with
Vena Copilot using simple, natural language to get fast insights, generate reports and make
data-driven decisions on the fly. Built for FP&A, Vena Copilot offers tailored prompts and
guidance to support budget owners and business leaders. With Vena Copilot, you can:
Share insights and reports directly in Teams channels or group chats.
Bring Vena Copilot into meetings as your “analyst in the room” to answer questions, surface
insights and make real-time recommendations.
Collaborate live on reports using Excel and Vena’s Ad Hoc reporting tool.
With Vena Copilot in Microsoft Teams, your planning conversations become more insightful,
collaborative and actionable – in real time.

FAQ - Vena Copilot in Microsoft Teams
Are all Vena Copilot features available in Microsoft Teams?
All the end user features that are available in the web version of Vena Copilot are available in
Microsoft Teams. This means you can chat with Vena Copilot to generate insights and reports
the same way.

Can I train Vena Copilot from Microsoft Teams?
As an end user, you can provide feedback on insights and reports in Microsoft Teams that will be
stored for use for further training. For insights queries, end users can use the thumbs up or
down icons to provide feedback. For reports, if the report matches your expectations for the
Vena Copilot query, select Yes. If the report doesn’t match your expectations, select Needs
Adjustment.
AI Model Managers cannot train AI models in Teams. Navigate to the Builder tab in vena.io to
add Prompts and train Vena Copilot.
How do I log in to Vena Copilot for Microsoft Teams with SSO?
When opening the Vena Copilot for Microsoft Teams app, enter your Vena Account ID and select
Log In. You'll then be redirected to your SSO provider to complete the login process.

Is my Vena Copilot tab private?
Yes, your Vena Copilot tab is always private in Microsoft Teams, even in meetings. Other users
may be able to see the tab, but each user has a private Vena Copilot chat.
What can other meeting participants see in an Excel Live session?
As the presenter, only you can see the Vena Ad Hoc task pane in the report. All other
participants can only see the report being shared.
Can I close the report that I generated in the first step before starting an Excel
Live session?
Yes, you can close the report, whether you have it opened in a separate Teams window or
downloaded. Starting an Excel Live session generates a new report that is independent of the
report generated in the previous step. Vena Copilot loads the same data into the new report, so
you can be confident you're sharing the correct data with meeting participants.

How does Vena Copilot integrate with Excel Live?
Excel Live is a collaborative feature in Microsoft Teams that allows meeting participants to edit
and explore Excel reports from the Teams window.
After generating a report in a chat with Vena Copilot, you can launch Excel Live to share the
report with meeting participants. The presenter can leverage Vena Ad Hoc features to slice and
dice data on the fly. All meeting participants can collaboratively edit data during the session as
well.
Why can't I find my report in OneDrive?
After generating a report with Vena Copilot in Microsoft Teams and selecting Open in Teams,
your file will appear in the Excel Online app as a recent file. However, it won't appear as a recent
file in OneDrive. Search for the file by name to locate it in OneDrive.
Why do I need to grant Vena permission to access my OneDrive?
Vena requires permission to access your OneDrive and store reports generated and downloaded
from Vena Copilot.  Vena only receives permission to one folder in your OneDrive.
